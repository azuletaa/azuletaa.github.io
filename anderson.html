Crime and punishment

Considered by critics as Dostoevsky's first masterpiece, Crime and Punishment is a profound psychological analysis of its protagonist, the young student Raskolnikov, whose firm belief that humanitarian ends justify evil leads him to the murder of a loan shark. But from the moment he commits the crime, guilt will be a constant nightmare with which the student will be unable to live.

Take a great adventure with one of the most important works of world literature with the famous translation by Rafael Cansinos Assens, revised and modernized for the occasion. It is also accompanied by an introduction by David McDuff, translator and literary critic specializing in the author's work.

Media Literacy

We invite you to deepen in this set of competencies related to the interpretation, management and handling of information and media and socio-digital content, which focuses on the literacy development of different skills and knowledge that manage to transform the types of interaction that people make and have with the informational and media of their environments.

"The program has two main objectives: on the one hand, to help people understand how the media works today, how it uses algorithms and discourses to generate trends, violence or disinformation. On the other hand, to encourage the active participation of people, especially young people, to intervene in the messages and counteract disinformation with truthful data, empathy and substantive issues," says Adolfo Rodriguez.
